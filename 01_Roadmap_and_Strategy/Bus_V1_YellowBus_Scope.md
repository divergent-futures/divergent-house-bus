# Bus V1 - the "Yellow Bus" Proof-of-Concept: Scope

**Date:** 2026-06-29  ·  **Stage 1** of the build ladder (Slipstream -> **V1** -> V2). Part of Divergent Futures - Terrestrial Technology.

## What V1 is (and isn't)
V1 is a **school bus converted to electric**, built to **integrate as much of the V2 vision as off-the-shelf parts allow** - not a stripped EV skoolie, and not the full bespoke thesis. It is:
- A bus **we actually want to live in and travel the country in**, running on the **electric charging infrastructure that already exists**.
- A **skills-builder and a channel** - the point is to *show the Divergent Futures community that it can be done*, that the lifestyle is real and the engineering/science checks out.
- **Off-the-shelf where possible** (no bespoke structural pack, no roboforming, no custom thermal core). We accept it will be **less efficient and harder to charge** than V2 - that's part of the journey, and it's honest, watchable content.
- A **reduced feature set**: the essentials and the experience, not every luxury. (Composting toilet with urine separator - yes. Sauna, full hydroponics green wall, closed-loop reticulating shower - **deferred to V2**.)
- Possibly **Kickstarter-funded**, started on-channel to "see how it goes."

V1 proves the concept and builds the audience; **V2 is the bespoke, fully-integrated payoff** (structural sodium pack, all-DC, CO2 thermal core).

## Subsystem map - V1 (off-the-shelf) vs V2 (the north star)

| Subsystem | **V1 - off-the-shelf** | V2 - bespoke (north star) |
|---|---|---|
| **Platform/structure** | Donor **school bus**; pack **carried** (not structural); conventional spray-foam insulation; existing body | Bespoke structural-pack space-frame, roboformed enclosure, aerogel/VIP, composite panels |
| **Drivetrain** | Off-the-shelf / salvage EV drivetrain (e.g. crate motor or salvage e-axle); sized for highway hops | Tiered sourced/bespoke e-axle, not oversized |
| **Battery** | **Off-the-shelf LFP or salvage EV modules**, carried; modest kWh (whatever budget/range allows); Orion-class BMS | ~330 kWh structural **sodium** dual-domain (400 V + 48 V), 800 V-ready |
| **Charging** | **Existing infrastructure** - off-the-shelf CCS/NACS onboard charging; lower power, slower, "harder" - accepted | MCS-ready + NACS, high-power |
| **Electrical** | **Conventional off-the-shelf 48 V + inverter** (Victron-class) - simpler, accept the inversion loss | **All-DC** (48/24/12 V + USB-C), no main inverter |
| **Thermal / HVAC** | Off-the-shelf **mini-split heat pump** + **diesel air/water heater** (Webasto/Espar) for winter; standard **DC fridge/freezer** | Integrated **CO2 dual-circuit core** with grade-matched heat reuse |
| **Cooking** | Off-the-shelf induction + small oven/air-fryer (on the inverter) | 48 V DC induction + DC convection oven |
| **Water** | **Composting toilet + urine separator**; fresh + grey tanks; basic filter/UV | Closed-loop reticulating shower + condensate + urine->hydroponics |
| **Solar** | Off-the-shelf roof panels + MPPT | Roof + deployable 5-10 kW array |
| **Controls** | Off-the-shelf (Victron Cerbo/VRM + simple) | Bespoke automotive-Ethernet backbone, phone-as-key |
| **Interior** | Bed, galley, lounge, **filming/work space**; comfortable, not maximal | Full amenity set (sauna, hydroponics wall, climate bed, etc.) |

## Why this is the right V1 (Divergent Futures framing)
- **It's buildable now** with parts you can actually buy - no waiting on bespoke fabrication or supplier relationships.
- **It earns the right to build V2:** skills, an audience, a track record, and possibly crowdfunding - and every off-the-shelf compromise becomes a clear "here's how V2 does it better" story.
- **It demonstrates the thesis at the lowest risk:** that an all-electric, decoupled, full-time mobile dwelling is *possible today* - the science checks out, on camera.

## Conversion vs ground-up: what's actually worth keeping (feasibility, 2026-06-29)

The ship-of-Theseus test: **a donor is only worth what you KEEP.** Strip it to bare frame rails - remove the diesel drivetrain, all wiring/CAN, swap the axles, *and* replace the heavy steel body - and you've paid to buy a bus **and** to gut it, keeping almost nothing. At that point a donor is a liability, not a head start.

Two things make a donor genuinely worth keeping, and a **light-touch repower preserves both**:
1. **The steel body/shell** - you don't have to design and build a body (a huge task in a ground-up build).
2. **The road-legal title/VIN** - a *converted EV* **keeps its original title, VIN, line-make, and model year** as long as you only change what's needed for electric propulsion (then a safety inspection; state-dependent). A **ground-up build is a "specially constructed vehicle
