# Two Build Variants — Standard vs Signature (both open source)

**Date:** 2026-07-03  ·  V2 bus, strategy + packaging. TJ's fork: there are **two buses** — one **realistic** build for everyone else, and TJ's own **thesis-demonstrator** build that pushes the integration further. **Both are open source.** First concrete divergence = the **wet room**. See `06_.../wet_room_two_variants.png`. Feeds floor-plan (two wet-zone layouts) + the tier line + personas.

---

## 1. The fork
| | **STANDARD** (realistic) | **SIGNATURE** (TJ's demonstrator) |
|---|---|---|
| Purpose | The buildable design **anyone** can replicate | The full-thesis showcase — how far the integration *can* go |
| Ethos | Adopt-off-the-shelf, simple, robust, low-cost | Extrapolate every permutation; clever, integrated, transforming |
| Buyer | Entry / Mid tiers (retirees, contractors, creators, priced-out) | The reference/halo build (content, proof, TJ himself) |
| Risk | Low — fewer moving parts, fewer failure points | Higher — mechanisms, seals, more to maintain |

This maps onto the project's standing philosophy: *extrapolate every permutation (Signature) but adopt-off-the-shelf where a simple solution genuinely fits (Standard).* Publishing both means a builder picks the complexity they want.

## 2. First divergence — the wet room
**STANDARD:** one **small, fixed combined wet room** — a compact shower + composting toilet in the same space. No moving parts. Simple, robust, cheap; anyone can build it.

**SIGNATURE:** a **reconfigurable wet room** that multi-purposes a small footprint by sliding parts:
- **Stowed (living / growing mode):** the **hydroponic system** occupies the wet-zone alongside a small cabinet; the **composting toilet is stowed on rails under a galley cabinet**; the shower is folded away.
- **Bathroom mode:** the **hydro slides aside** — into the **rear-bedroom edge**, displacing a small cabinet / a slice of the bedroom — which **frees the wet room so the shower becomes usable**; the **composting toilet slides out on rails** from under the galley cabinet into the wet room.
- **After use:** toilet slides back under the galley cabinet; hydro slides back in.

So one small footprint does **bathroom + growing + storage** by time-sharing the volume between the galley, the wet room, and a sliver of the bedroom.

## 3. The consideration (honest trade)
- **Signature** buys space/function density at the cost of **mechanism complexity** — rails, seals, and the non-trivial problem of **moving a (composting) toilet** and wet fixtures repeatedly. More to maintain, more to get right; a demonstrator, not a mass product.
- **Standard** trades that space-sharing for **reliability and simplicity** — the thing the target buyer (esp. retirees) actually wants, and the thing mass-market RVs fail at.
- **Bedroom impact (Signature):** the hydro's stow position eats a small portion of the rear bedroom — fold into the bedroom packaging when the wet-room section is drawn.
