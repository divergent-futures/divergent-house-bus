# Food & Hydroponics Capacity — What 3 People Can Realistically Grow

**Date:** 2026-07-03  ·  V2 bus (Signature). Honest agronomic sizing of the 35 × 35 in hydro stack: what to grow, how much it yields, height + lighting requirements, and what fraction of 3 people's diet it actually covers. See `food_hydro_capacity.png`. Feeds the energy model (grow-light load), Water track (nutrient loop), and the honest thesis framing.

---

## 1. The honest headline (no overpromising)
A 35 × 35 in × ~5-tier hydro stack gives 3 people a **daily fresh-greens + microgreen + herb supply** — real, high-value nutrition, food security, and morale — but it is **not** a source of **calories, protein, or staples**. Those come from **stored/bought dry goods** (rice, beans, oats, oil, tinned/dry protein). The bus grows the **fresh, perishable, nutrient-dense** part of the diet; it stores the **dense-energy** part.

## 3. What it covers for 3 people
- **Fresh vegetables (by volume): ~30%** of the ~400 g/person/day target.
- **Vitamins A / C / K / folate + antioxidants: a large share** (greens + microgreens are dense in these; microgreens carry **4–40×** the mature plant's nutrients).
- **Calories: <2%.** Leafy greens are ~15–25 kcal/100 g.
- **Protein / staples: ~0%** — must be stored/bought.

## 4. Best crops (nutrition × bus-viability), ranked
1. **Microgreens** (broccoli, radish, pea, sunflower, kale) — fastest (**7–14 days**), densest nutrition, **low height** (more tiers), tolerate the motion, highest yield per area. *The top pick for a moving bus.*
2. **Leafy greens** (kale, chard, spinach, lettuce, arugula) — vitamins A/C/K/folate, iron, calcium; easy hydroponically, continuous cut-and-come-again harvest.
3. **High-density greens** (watercress, bok choy, tatsoi, mizuna) — watercress is one of the most nutrient-dense foods (ANDI #1) and loves hydroponics; Asian greens are fast and compact.
4. **Sprouts** (mung, lentil, alfalfa) — grown **in the dark** (no light, ~zero energy) in a galley drawer; fast, a little protein.

**Herbs (basil, cilantro, parsley, mint, chives)** grow in a **separate small kitchen planter by the galley** (window + a small LED), not in this stack — freeing the tier for higher-yield greens.

**Avoid (poor fit):** fruiting crops (tomato, pepper, cucumber) — need height, high light, **months**, pollination, and they **dislike the motion**; and root/tuber (carrot, potato) — space-heavy, not hydroponic-friendly.

## 5. Lighting & energy (feeds the energy model)
- **Leafy/herbs:** DLI **~12 mol/m²/day** at PPFD **~200–250 µmol/m²/s**, ~15 h photoperiod. Energy efficiency ~**206 g FW/kWh** at the optimal DLI.
- **Microgreens:** lower light + **2–3 blackout (germination) days** per cycle → less energy.
- **Total ~3.3 kWh/day** for the full stack. **Solar-covered in summer**; in winter it's a **real load (~20–30% of the ~10–15 kWh/day house use)** — a deliberate trade for fresh food. (Theoretical optimized ceiling from the energy figure is ~20 kg/mo; ~11 kg is a realistic home-system estimate — headroom exists if lit harder.)
- **Motion note:** greens/microgreens tolerate the daily slide + road motion; fruiting crops would not — another reason the crop list is greens-first.
