# Length & Size vs Access - the length decision

**Date:** 2026-06-29  ·  V2 bus. Sets the length before floor-plan v0.3. Access-driven, not preference-driven.

## The national-park campground access curve (the decisive data)
Share of NPS campgrounds that accommodate an RV of a given length:

| Length | NPS campgrounds that fit |
|---|---|
| <= 19 ft | ~98% |
| **<= 25 ft** | **93%** |
| <= 29 ft | ~84% |
| <= 35 ft | ~73% |
| >= 40 ft | ~53% |

Average NPS length limit ~27 ft. **The knee is at ~25 ft: it captures 93% of campgrounds, and every foot beyond loses access fast (25 -> 29 ft drops ~9 points).** Below 25 ft the gains shrink (23 ft ~= 95%) while you lose precious living length - so 25 ft is the efficient point, not 23.

## Parking
- **Standard stall:** ~18-20 ft. **Parallel:** ~19-22 ft (up to 24). **Oversized:** up to ~25 ft.
- A **25 ft** bus fits a long parallel / oversized space (~25 ft) or ~2 standard stalls end-to-end; a **27 ft** just exceeds the typical 25 ft oversized stall (noticeably worse to park); **29 ft** is firmly RV/truck-parking only. Parking alone argues for **<= 25 ft**.

## The extreme park roads are the toad's job, not the bus's
The tightest iconic roads are stricter than any reasonable dwelling: **Going-to-the-Sun (Glacier) = <=21 ft length, <=8 ft width (incl mirrors), ~10 ft height**; several others similar. Our bus (~25 ft, ~98 in wide, ~8.4 ft tall) **won't fit those on length or width regardless** - and it doesn't need to: the **towed 4WD "toad" handles the tight scenic roads** while the bus stays parked at a campground/lot. So bus length is set by **campgrounds + parking + general roads**, not the extreme roads. (Shrinking the dwelling to 21 ft to chase Going-to-the-Sun would be the wrong trade - that's what the toad is for.)

## Width & height (already fine)
- **Width:** body ~2.5 m (~98 in) is **under the 102 in federal max** (legal on interstates). Some secondary/park roads restrict to 96 in (8 ft) - the toad covers those; keep the body <= 102 in and ideally near ~96-98 in. Slide-outs give the *parked* width without adding travel width.
- **Height:** ~8.40 ft - **far under the 13.5 ft limit** and even under the ~10 ft park-road overhang concerns. No issue (a big benefit of the low battery-floor profile).

## Recommendation: **25 ft**
Lock the bus at **25 ft** (from the earlier 25-27 ft range):
- Sits **exactly on the 93% campground-access knee** - the most access any livable length can buy.
- Fits **oversized/long-parallel parking** (~25 ft) and ~2 standard stalls.
- Every foot past 25 (to 27/29) **costs access + parking** for modest extra room; every foot below 25 **costs living space** for little access gain.
- The **toad** covers the sub-21 ft scenic roads, so we don't shrink the dwelling for them.
- **27 ft is the only "more room" fallback** - it keeps ~most campgrounds (~88%) but loses easy parking; take it only if the floor-plan proves 25 ft too tight.

*(Per the scalability note, 25 ft is the reference platform; longer variants exist for builders who prioritise room over access.)*

## Feeds
Draw **floor-plan v0.3 at 25 ft** interior-length budget. Revisit only if the layout can't fit the essentials at 25 ft (then 27 ft, with the access cost noted).
