## 1. What this project is (consolidated)

A full-time, four-season, **all-electric self-moving dwelling** — "the thing that comes after the RV and the tiny house." Not a camper you return home from; the bus *is* the home. It carries a personal goal (a dwelling the operator and his wife actually want to live in) and a Divergent Futures thesis (*decoupling the dwelling from the land*) at the same time. The engineering signature is **integration over invention**: one structural battery, one thermal loop, closed-loop water, and waste-heat reuse, amalgamating technologies that exist separately but have never been tied together.

The build philosophy mirrors HABITAT: prove the integrated concept on a converted **school bus (V1 prototype)**, then carry the validated architecture into a purpose-built **ground-up vehicle (V2)**.

## 3. The current architecture, as the documents describe it

**Energy core.** A single **structural high-voltage traction battery** is the only energy reservoir — it drives the bus *and* runs the house, eliminating a separate house bank. Latest docs converge on **~300 kWh LFP Blade cells** acting as the chassis spine (battery-as-structure) with a **48 V DC house bus** stepped down once via a 5–10 kW DC-DC converter. The 48 V house side is locked (TE Connectivity's 48 V connector portfolio is treated as the enabling technology). A small **110/120 V AC inverter** (3–6 kW) covers only the few legacy loads.

**Propulsion.** Full battery-electric drivetrain: single e-axle (~200–250 kW), ~300-mile target range, NACS + MCS charging, aero + adjustable air suspension. This is core identity, not optional.

**Thermal — the integration heart.** One closed glycol loop, Tesla-Octovalve-style multi-port routing, serving cabin HVAC (heat pump), battery conditioning, fridge/freezer, **domestic hot water from compressor waste heat**, a ventless heat-pump dryer, and even a **climate-controlled "Sleep 8"-style bed** tapped off the same loop. A **CHP genset** is the affirmed winter/off-grid backstop — its waste heat is the product, not a loss.

**Water & loops.** Closed-loop recirculating shower (85–95% reuse, ~10–15 gal total inventory), AC condensate / AWG as a near-distilled water source for hydroponics, urine-diverting composting toilet / electric food recycler, greywater to hydroponics. Bounded honestly as a *demonstrator + fresh greens*, not food self-sufficiency.

**Controls.** Linux host (Python, automotive Ethernet + CAN gateways) + optional Windows VM, Raspberry Pi/ESP32 edge nodes, Home Assistant, Victron VRM. Priority-based load shedding with a protected **drive-reserve floor**. **The "brain" (v0.3, `08_.../Controls_Brain_Orchestration_v0_3.md`): a 6-layer stack — L0 sensors, L1 deterministic SAFETY (never overridden), L2 subsystem controllers, L3 supervisory energy manager (load-shed ladder + power-flow arbitration + whole-vehicle STATE MACHINE + microgrid coordination), L4 PREDICTIVE MPC (plans 24-48 h: charge/thermal/grow/V2X/level; min diesel + max comfort s.t. always-reach-destination; learns your patterns), L5 HMI/app/OTA/community. Smart layers optimize, safety guarantees; on smart-layer loss all subsystems fall to safe local control. Split-brain compute + digital twin + V1-reactive→V2-MPC phasing.** **Buildable spec (v0.4, `08_.../Controls_Brain_Spec_v0_4.md`): the dynamic drive-reserve formula `E_reserve=(D_safe·e_drive·k_wx·k_terrain+E_climb)·(1+m)` (breathes ~55–170 kWh with conditions, not a flat placeholder); the DRIVE-entry interlock (slides in + tray stowed + dock off + ride-height + reserve met) + full state-machine transition table; the MPC formulation (48 h/15 min, objective + inviolable `SOC≥E_reserve` constraint); and the sensor/redundancy map (air-quality/HV/SOC redundant; DRIVE-guard sensors assume-unsafe-on-loss).** **Digital twin (v0.5, `08_.../Digital_Twin_v0_5.md` + runnable `digital_twin/`): a 1-h-step energy/SOC simulation with the reactive controller that verifies 3 safety invariants (parked SOC≥reserve, never stranded, SOC≥5%). It caught 3 of our own model errors — the reserve rate (1.6) was wrongly used as actual driving consumption (→ split to ~1.2 actual / ~1.3 planning; SHOULD PROPAGATE to range/tier), CHP waste heat wasn't crediting cabin heat (winter diesel ~9.7→~0–4 L/day), and climb energy was free. Baselines all safe; break-tests 5/6 held (14-day blackout, forced over-export self-limits at the reserve floor, −25 °C snap); the 6th documents a real limit (big-climb backcountry = charge-stop trip). See `digital_twin_results.png`.**

**Pack capacity — nameplate vs usable (`03_.../Pack_Capacity_Usable_and_BMS_Window.md` + `08_.../pack_capacity_stack.png`):** High tier **390 kWh nameplate → ~351 kWh daily-usable** (charge ceiling ~95 %, app-0 % at ~5 %) + a hidden **~8–12 kWh survival reserve** (2–5 %) for critical loads only. **Two distinct reserves:** the fixed chemistry/BMS window (top buffer + survival sliver, protects cells) vs the dynamic operational **drive-reserve** (get-home energy inside the usable window, ~55–170 kWh, enforced by L1) — don't confuse them. **Sodium makes the wide window safe:** 0 V-safe at 100 % DOD with no mechanical degradation, safe 0 V storage, 10k–20k-cycle cells, crush-safe — so the bottom buffer is a *design choice*, not a chemistry cap. **Correction:** quote range on *usable* (~351 → ~290 mi at 1.2 kWh/mi), not nameplate; tier figures are nameplate and need usable+range each (v0.6).

**The genset is a SERIES range-extender + CHP, not a hybrid (`04_.../Genset_Series_RangeExtender_and_Cold_Preconditioning.md` + `08_.../genset_series_and_cold_chp.png`):** the diesel engine has no mechanical path to the wheels — it makes electricity + heat only; the **battery alone drives the wheels** (BEV identity holds). Connection = **Option A**: genset rectifies to the **48 V house bus** (like solar), the existing **bidirectional DC-DC** lifts surplus to the 400 V pack; the CHP is small (~3.5 kW, job = hold-reserve + precondition, not bulk-charge). **Cold-weather multiplier:** one genset run does 6 jobs at once — charge, cabin heat, hot water, warm pipes, **precondition the pack so it accepts regen** (cold pack curtails regen; ~40 % range loss at 20 °F; preconditioning recovers 20–30 %), and melt snow off the panels. **Twin (10 d @ −12 °C):** the CHP delivers ~16 kWh/day of heat for **~0.9 L/day** with comfort met and reserve held; without it ~181 kWh of heat is shed (cold cabin) — so you "lose less battery" with the genset. Per ~1 L diesel: ~35 % electricity + ~55 % usable heat ≈ **~90 % fuel utilization**. APU-agnostic (a cleaner range-extender can drop in).

## 5. How the three starter deliverables relate

The starter docs were built before these sources were available, on deliberately generic assumptions. Reconciliation:

- **`House_BUS_Systems_Architecture.docx`** — its thesis (heat is never wasted; one thermal loop feeds hot water, fridge, drying, cabin, battery) **matches your TMS / Octovalve vision well**. It generalises cleanly; mainly needs the shared-traction-pack framing and the 48 V/HV split added.
- **`House_BUS_Energy_Model.xlsx`** — **re-based (v2, 2026-06-25)** on the locked decisions: shared **300 kWh** structural pack (90% usable, 80 kWh drive-reserve floor → **190 kWh house-available**), full-amenity loads (~**10.3 kWh/day**), roof+deployable solar (**6 kW parked**), and the **3.5 kW diesel CHP** in a ski-weekend winter. Headline results: summer solar runs a **+11 kWh/day surplus**; the pack alone gives **~13 days** of winter autonomy at −10 °C, so the genset is only needed for longer/colder stays (**~2.3 h/day, ~2.6 L diesel** to hold the pack indefinitely); driving range **~245 mi**.
- **`House_BUS_Master_Roadmap.docx`** — structure (phases, gates, risks) is reusable, but it must absorb: full EV propulsion as core, the 25-vs-35 ft and 180-vs-300 kWh decisions, the toad, closed-loop water, and the Divergent Futures framing.

*Note:* "Sleep 8" refers to an **Eight Sleep-style climate-controlled bed**, not eight occupants — occupancy still reads as **2 (operator + wife)** from the concept doc. Good news: the 2-occupant basis in the energy model stands.
